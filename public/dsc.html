What is a Digital Signature Certificate?

A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is a secure digital key issued by a Certifying Authority that allows individuals and organizations to sign documents electronically. It ensures the authenticity, integrity, and non-repudiation of digital documents.

Because business registration is done online, all parties involved in the company must have digital signatures. They can put on the various papers that need to be submitted via the MCA portal during the registration process.

Why Do You Need a Digital Signature Certificate?

Legal Compliance

Required for MCA filings, tax returns, and other regulatory submissions.

Security

Provides secure authentication and prevents document tampering.

Efficiency

Streamlines online processes and reduces paperwork.

Types of Digital Signature Certificates

Class 2 DSC

For individuals and businesses. Used for filing income tax returns, GST returns, and company registrations.

Class 3 DSC

Higher security level. Required for e-tendering, patent filings, and high-value transactions.
